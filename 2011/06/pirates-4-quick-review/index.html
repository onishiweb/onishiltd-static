Pirates 4: quick review

Posted on 5th June, 2011, in Personal

Yep that’s right, 2 in one night! Look at me getting all typey and what-not! In truth the other one was written a couple of nights ago but today I just went over it again and re-wrote the start and end so not really a full post. And this one’s not going to be very long either just a quick attempt at writing a review as I’ve just gotten home from seeing Pirates of the Caribbean 4: On Stranger Tides. N.B. This does contain spoilers so if you haven’t see the film yet, leave now!

I saw the film in 2D on the recommendation of my mum who’s been to see it twice already and said that it doesn’t really hold up in 3D but having not seen it I can’t comment (I’m a bit of a fan of 3D and would like to see it but as she was paying for her third trip, I wasn’t going to complain). I’d seen a couple of trailers for the film in both 2 and 3 dimensions and both looked equally as spectacular so I was pretty expectant going into the film…

A lesser than grand arrival

One thing I’ve always liked about the Pirates films is that Captain Jack Sparrow always makes an entrance, be it the sinking ship coming into port in the first or shooting his way out of a coffin in the second it’s always a grand and entertaining entrance. Unfortunately however this entrance was a bit too predictable and rather dull…you could see it coming from the minute Gibbs was being mistaken for Jack in the court.

As usual the film was full of brilliant fight scenes throughout although the first one on one sword fight with the fake Jack Sparrow was very much like the sword fight with Will in the first movie, fighting on the beams again!

The movie took off more when Black Beard was introduced during the mutiny, a good addition as a new character to the series Black Beard had a similar effect to proceedings as Barbossa had in the first film, although not a downside like the sword fight towards the beginning.

The kiss of a mermaid

The best scene came with the mermaids, something the trails had made a thing of and certainly were for a good reason. The look of them, their fierceness and the very different take on a battle scene was spectacular and easily the best part of the film overall.

The story ends with a reoccurring theme from the movies of the desert island with a pistol and a shot but this time it’s not Jack being left but the girl which took me a little by surprise as it could have been quite a love story for Jack but I guess that they couldn’t leave it too obvious… Although meeting up with Gibbs again and getting the Pearl back was rather predictable.

An after credits tease leaves the series open for another film which I’ll be honest although this one did lack something of the others it was still a great film and I would happily see it again (in 3D obviously) and would be happy to see the story continue and see what more Jack can get up to…

Cut

Well there goes, that was my first attempt at a movie review, thought it would be a lot shorter than that… Although it seemed I was being rather negative towards the film I still loved it as with the rest of the Pirates films, I just feel it wasn’t the best of them. Anyway I’m now going to get another cup of tea and sit in front of the TV for the night before heading back to work tomorrow boo!

Night
